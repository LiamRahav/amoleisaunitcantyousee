What Is a Mole?

A mole is a unit that is used to express an amount of something. It is represented as Avogadro's Number, 6.02 x 1023, and is derived from the number of atoms in 12 grams of Carbon-12. A mole of any atom will contain the same mass in grams as it does in atomic mass units. The word "mole" comes form the German word "molekulargewicht", meaning "molecular weight". In the same fashion that you can have one apple, two apples, or a dozen apples, you can also have a mole of apples.

If a mole of McDonald’s Chicken McNuggets was to be evenly distributed between everyone in the world, how long would it take for all of the Chicken McNuggets to be eaten?

In order to figure out the answer to this question, the following equation was used: 6.02 x 1023 nuggets 7,395,051,488 people x 48.25 calories 1 nugget x 1 day 2,534 calories And provided the result of 1.55 x 1012 days. How long would it take to burn off a mole of McDonald’s Chicken McNuggets on a treadmill?

In order to figure out the answer to this question, the following equation was used: 6.02 x 1023 nuggets x 48.25 calories 1 nugget x 30 minutes 408 calories x 1 day 1,440 minutes And provided a result of 1.48 x 1021 days running on a treadmill.
