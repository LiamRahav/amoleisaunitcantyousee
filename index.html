What Is a Mole?

A mole is a unit that is used to express an amount of something. It is represented as Avogadro's Number, 6.02 x 1023, and is derived from the number of atoms in 12 grams of Carbon-12. A mole of any atom will contain the same mass in grams as it does in atomic mass units. The word "mole" comes form the German word "molekulargewicht", meaning "molecular weight". In the same fashion that you can have one apple, two apples, or a dozen apples, you can also have a mole of apples.

If a mole of McDonald’s Chicken McNuggets was to be evenly distributed between everyone in the world, how long would it take for all of the Chicken McNuggets to be eaten?

In order to figure out the answer to this question, the following equation was used: 6.02 x 10 23 nuggets 7,395,051,488 people x 48.25 calories 1 nugget x 1 day 2,534 calories And provided the result of 1.55 x 1012 days. That's an enormous amount of Chicken McNuggets! All of the statistics we used are cited in the footer of this website.

How long would it take to burn off a mole of McDonald’s Chicken McNuggets on a treadmill?

In order to figure out the answer to this question, the following equation was used: 6.02 x 10 23 nuggets x 48.25 calories 1 nugget x 30 minutes 408 calories x 1 day 1,440 minutes And provided a result of 1.48 x 1021 days running on a treadmill. Way to work up a sweat! Same as above, all citations and statistics are linked in the footer.

How many Chicken McNuggets can be created with a mole of sodium?

Assuming we would have an unlimited amount of other ingredients, the following equation was used: 90mg Na 1 nugget x 1g Na 1000mg Na x 1 mol Na 22.989g Na This provided us with the amount of moles of sodium per nugget: 0.003914916 moles Na. To find how many nuggets we could make with a single mol of Na, we divided both sides by 0.003914916 and got 260 nuggets per mole Na. A mole isn't quite so massive when you look at it on the atomic level!
